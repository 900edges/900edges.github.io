Research

I'm working with the intersection of large-graph limits, variational calculus, and nonlocal analysis. My current research involves graph Ginzburg-Landau theory and nonlocal perimeter. This research has applications in graph cut problems, image segmentation and social network analysis, among other questions. This research is in progress with Mason Porter, James Scott, and Qiang Du.

I'm also working on providing theoretical guaranteees for variational inference, a popular statistical inference algorithm. This research is in progress with Kaizheng Wang.

Publications

On Representations of Mean-Field Variational Inference (preprint)
  • We express variational inference as an approximate Wasserstein gradient flow.
Unveiling Mode-Connectivity of the ELBO Landscape (Workshop paper)
  • Mode-connectivity is an interesting property that has been discovered in neural net loss functions, which are high-dimensional and nonconvex. It has been related to a "no bad local minima" property that's been noticed in practice, and is related to overparametrization. This workshop paper demonstrates and begins to explain the existence of mode-connectivity in the ELBO, which is the loss function of variational inference.
